Region · United States

United States

🇺🇸
United States
20 brands · Sport · Casual · Premium · Dept Store · Mass Market
Revenue

13 of 20 US brands grew FY25. Premium and value outperformed; mid-market department stores Kohl's and Macy's declined every quarter. Tariff headwinds on Asia-sourced goods emerged as a shared H2 cost pressure across nearly every brand.

Income & Margins

Nike income collapsed from $5.70B → $3.22B (–44%). Under Armour posted a –$187M net loss. Promotional discounting, freight costs, and wage inflation compressed margins. Winners: Ralph Lauren (record 68.5% gross margin), Victoria's Secret (first full profitable year).

Inventory

Nike led inventory reduction at –$620M (7.52→6.90B), protecting margins. Levi's and AEO also cut sharply. Lululemon inventory edged up while revenue stalled — a watch point. Brands that discounted to clear excess (AEO Q1, Columbia) saw immediate margin pain.

Debt

VF Corp ($5.43B) carries the highest leverage risk — sold Dickies $600M to deleverage. Macy's ($2.78B) managing decline. Nike debt rose to $12.87B. Ralph Lauren, Levi's, and AEO all reduced debt YoY.

Region · Europe

Europe

🇪🇺
Europe
5 brands · Sport · Fast Fashion · Premium/Accessible Luxury
Revenue

Widest spread of any region. Adidas best year in a decade (+5.5%, EBIT +54%) on Samba/Originals supercycle. Inditex accelerated to +10.5% in Q4. PUMA collapsed –14.1% — worst in company history. H&M barely grew +0.9%. Hugo Boss declined all three reported quarters.

Income & Margins

Adidas net income ~€1.6B — first positive in two years, EBIT +54% to €2.06B. PUMA swung to a record €643.6M net loss — channel restructuring, impairments, and deep discounting. H&M best operating margin in 6 years (8.1%) via cost discipline, not volume.

Inventory

H&M cut $450M of inventory (3.67→3.22B) — quality-over-quantity strategy delivering. Adidas modest +4% build for planned growth. PUMA cleared stock via deep discounting — contributing directly to the record net loss. Inditex maintained just-in-time discipline all year.

Debt

Cleanest balance sheets globally. Adidas reduced debt 2.70→2.55B and launched €500M buyback. H&M cut 0.48→0.42B. Inditex near-zero net debt. PUMA and Hugo Boss debt stable but record losses degraded interest coverage ratios meaningfully.

Region · Canada

Canada

🇨🇦
Canada
2 brands · Athleisure (Lululemon) · Everyday Luxury (Aritzia)
Revenue

Aritzia: fastest-growing brand in the entire dataset — +20.2% FY25, double-digit every quarter. US flagships (Manhattan, Chicago) + DTC digital drove it. Lululemon decelerated from +7.2% Q1 to flat Q3 as US comparable traffic turned negative. China International +33% buffered but couldn't offset.

Income & Margins

Aritzia EBITDA +121.8% on revenue +20% — exceptional operating leverage. FY26 guidance raised to $3.05–3.25B. Lululemon income tracked revenue deceleration; Q4/FY25 results due March 17, 2026. Key question: can International income offset US margin compression?

Inventory

Lululemon inventory edged up ($1.59→$1.64B) while revenue growth stalled. Watch point for Q4 margins. Aritzia inventory proportional with planned US openings. Both Canadian brands carry zero or near-zero debt — rare financial advantage.

Debt

Lululemon: zero debt — the only major brand in this report with a completely clean balance sheet. Aritzia added modest debt for US build-out but EBITDA +121.8% means leverage improved dramatically. Both have maximum flexibility for FY26 expansion.

Region · Japan

Japan

🇯🇵
Japan
1 brand · Fast Retailing (Uniqlo · GU · Theory)
Revenue

Fast Retailing $21.89B (+3.2%). Strong Q1 surge (+10.9%) from Uniqlo US expansion (50th US store) and Heattech 3.0 launch. Q2 dipped (–3.2%) on JPY seasonal weakness. Q3–Q4 normalised. Marni and Lemaire collaborations sold out globally, confirming fashion credibility.

Income & Margins

FY25 operating income $2.887B — a record, up from $2.645B (+9.2%). Better gross margins from lean inventory and fewer markdowns, partially offset by higher US labour costs. FY26 profit forecast raised after strong trading update.

Inventory

Inventory managed as a core strategic competency. Heattech seasonal build (Q1–Q2) then disciplined sell-down (Q3–Q4). Grew modestly $2.24→$2.31B in line with store expansion. No excess stock reported at any point. Zero net debt.

Debt

Zero net debt — three consecutive years of reduction driven by strong Uniqlo operating cash flow across ~2,400 global stores. One of two cleanest balance sheets in this report. Significant capacity for US and India expansion. FY26 guidance raised.
